# Peridot Help — Client Guide

This guide walks you through using Peridot Help as a customer, from creating your account to getting your questions answered. No technical knowledge required.

---

## 1. Sign up

1. Open the app's home page. If you're not logged in, you'll see a welcome box:
   **"Hey 👋 Let's start with your email."**
2. Click **Sign up** (link at the bottom of the form).
3. Enter your **email address** and a **password**.
4. Click **Get Started**.

You'll be signed in and taken straight to your tickets page. Your account is created as a **customer** automatically.

> Already have an account? Click **Sign in** instead and enter your email + password.

---

## 2. Log in

1. On the home page, the **Login** form is shown by default.
2. Enter your **email** and **password**.
3. Click **Login**.

If your details are wrong, an error message appears below the form. Fix it and try again.

---

## 3. Your home — Tickets

After logging in, you land on your **Tickets** page. Here you'll find:

- A friendly greeting: *"Hi! Looking good. 😊"*
- An **ASK ANYTHING** button to start a new conversation.
- A list of all your existing tickets, each showing its **title**, **status** (e.g. open, closed), **priority** (if not normal), and when it was created.

Click any ticket card to reopen that conversation.

### The sidebar

On the left edge there's a slim navigation bar with these icons (hover to see labels):

| Icon | Goes to |
|------|---------|
| Chat bubble | **Tickets** (home) |
| Folder | **Files** — everything you've attached |
| Gear | **Settings** — your account |
| Door / arrow | **Logout** |

---

## 4. Start a new request (open a ticket)

1. On the Tickets page, click **ASK ANYTHING**.
2. A new ticket is created and you're taken into its chat view.
3. The header shows **Peridot Support** and your ticket number/title.

You're now ready to chat.

---

## 5. Chat with support

Inside a ticket:

- Type your message in the **"Type a message"** box at the bottom.
- Press **Enter** to send.
- Your messages appear on one side, support replies on the other.
- New replies show up **live** — no need to refresh.
- Scroll up to load **older messages** in the conversation automatically.

### Attach a file

1. Click the **attachment (paperclip) icon** to the left of the message box.
2. Choose a file from your device.

**Supported file types:** images (PNG, JPEG, GIF, WebP), PDF, Word (.doc/.docx), Excel (.xls/.xlsx), CSV, and plain text (.txt).

**Size limit:** 10 MB per file. Larger files are rejected with a notice.

Images preview directly in the chat; other files appear as a document you (and support) can open.

---

## 6. View your files

Click the **folder icon** in the sidebar (or go to **My Files**) to see every file you've shared across all your tickets, in one table showing:

- A thumbnail (for images) or a document icon
- File **name**, **size**, **type**, and **upload date**
- **View** — opens the file in a new tab
- **Download** — saves the file to your device

If you haven't shared anything yet, you'll see: *"No files yet. Attach a file in a ticket and it will appear here."*

---

## 7. Settings & your account

Click the **gear icon** in the sidebar (or go to **Settings**) to see:

- Your **email**
- Your **Account ID**
- Your **Role** (customer)
- A **Sign out** button

---

## 8. Log out

Either:

- Click **Sign out** on the Settings page, or
- Use the **Logout** icon in the sidebar.

You'll be returned to the login screen.

---

## Quick tips

- **Keep one ticket per topic.** Start a new ticket (ASK ANYTHING) for a separate issue so conversations stay organized.
- **Replies are real-time.** Leave a ticket open and you'll see support respond live.
- **Your files are always retrievable** from the Files page, even after a ticket is closed.
- **Stuck on login?** Double-check your email spelling and password; error messages appear right under the form.

---

*Need anything else? Just open a ticket and Ask Anything — Peridot Support is here to help.*
